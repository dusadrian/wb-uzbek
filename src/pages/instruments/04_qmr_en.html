Material resources of the institution and the living conditions of the residing children

Questionnaire for childcare institutions and specialized boarding-schools

INTRODUCTION

The primary goal of this data collection is to support the Government of Uzbekistan in transitioning from institutional care to family-type care for children deprived of parental care. To achieve this, we aim to build a robust monitoring mechanism at the national level. By collecting detailed data, we can inform evidence-based decisions and policies that promote family-based alternatives.

Another critical objective is to enhance the quality of services provided to children with disabilities in institutional care. By understanding their unique needs and experiences, we can develop targeted interventions and improve their overall well-being. Additionally, we seek to explore and promote adequate family-type alternatives for these children.

The data collection on material and financial resources aims to assess the facilities, resources, and financial aspects of the child care institutions and boarding schools, in order to enhance the overall quality of child care services. The ultimate goal is to create safe, nurturing environments that promote the well-being and development of every child placed into care, guided by evidence.

PURPOSE: The data collected through this instrument contributes to the assessment of childcare institutions and specialized boarding schools in terms of the material resources available to them and the living conditions they provide to those living in these institutions.

How to complete: This questionnaire is to be completed individually for each institution operating at the time of the research (1 January 2024).

Accessibility standards that should be taken into consideration:

  • Barrier-Free Design: The building should be designed to accommodate individuals with various physical abilities. This includes features such as ramps, elevators, wide doorways, and accessible bathrooms.
  • Wheelchair Accessibility: All areas of the building, including common spaces, bedrooms, and outdoor areas, should be easily navigable by individuals using wheelchairs.
  • Visual and Auditory Accessibility: Consideration should be given to individuals with visual or auditory impairments. This may involve features like Braille signage, audible alarms, and clear visual cues.
  • Safety Measures: The building should have safety features in place, such as handrails, non-slip surfaces, and emergency exits that are accessible to all residents.
  • Clear Communication: Information within the building should be communicated clearly and effectively. This includes written materials, signage, and instructions.
